An Attempt to Walk Through Yale's Sculpture Garden On a Rainy Day

The rain provided me with the opportunity to view the sculpture garden through a new and unexpected lens: the indoors.

Read more about the Pyramid sculpture: Yale News Article

While I was looking through the glass window and attempting to view each sculpture in the garden, I had the chance to research each piece and learn more about its origin. As explained in the article above, this sculpture was built by Yale undergrads who had the chance to take a first year seminar that culminated in the creation of this pyramid. I originally thought my first attempt to walk through the sculpture garden was a fail and that there was no point in viewing a garden through glass, I now can see the benefit that I gained through having time to be curious about each piece.

A more Successful Walk Through Yale's Sculpture Garden!

The next time I approached the YUAG the weather was perfect. The garden was open again, and I could finally walk through, this time outside.

Right as I stepped into the sculpture garden, I felt the sun warm my face. I could see it reflect on the metal sculptures and onto the grass as I walked through.
